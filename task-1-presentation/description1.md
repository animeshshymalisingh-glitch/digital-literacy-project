# Task 1 – Digital Literacy Awareness Infographic

## Tool Used
**Canva** (canva.com) — a free, browser-based graphic design platform that requires no prior design experience.

## What the Infographic Shows

The infographic is titled **"Digital Literacy: Skills Every Student Needs"** and covers three core topics:

1. **What is Digital Literacy?** — Defined as the ability to find, evaluate, create, and communicate information using digital technologies responsibly and safely. Digital literacy goes beyond just knowing how to use a smartphone — it includes critical thinking about online information, data privacy, and professional digital conduct.

2. **Safe Internet Practices** — Includes using strong and unique passwords for every account, enabling two-factor authentication (2FA), avoiding suspicious links received via SMS or WhatsApp, and never sharing OTPs or personal details with unknown callers.

3. **Professional Online Presence** — Highlights the importance of setting up profiles on platforms like GitHub, LinkedIn, and Stack Overflow, keeping content professional, using a college email for academic sign-ups, and being mindful of what you post publicly.

## Design Approach
The infographic uses a clean blue-and-white colour scheme with bold section headers, icons, and short bullet points to make information easy to scan at a glance. It is sized for digital sharing — ideal for a WhatsApp group or classroom screen.

## Reflection
The most interesting part of this task was learning that infographic design is about *removing* information, not adding it. My first draft had too much text and looked cluttered. The most challenging part was deciding which three topics to include — digital literacy is a broad subject and there was much more worth covering than one page allows.
