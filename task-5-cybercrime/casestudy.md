# Case Study: UPI / Online Payment Fraud

## Overview
UPI (Unified Payments Interface) fraud is one of the fastest-growing cybercrimes in India, targeting students, working professionals, and senior citizens. Unlike traditional bank fraud, UPI scams often require no hacking — the victim is psychologically manipulated into authorising the transaction themselves.

## How It Typically Happens (Step-by-Step)

1. **The Setup:** The fraudster finds the victim's phone number — often through OLX, Facebook Marketplace, or a college group where the victim has posted something for sale or asked for financial help.
2. **First Contact:** The scammer calls or messages, posing as a buyer, scholarship officer, or bank employee. They claim to want to send money to the victim.
3. **The Trap:** The fraudster sends a *payment request* (not a payment) via a UPI app such as PhonePe, Google Pay, or Paytm. The interface looks like an incoming payment.
4. **Urgency & Deception:** The scammer urges the victim to "approve the payment quickly" or "enter your PIN to receive the money." The victim, believing they are *receiving* funds, enters their UPI PIN.
5. **Money Lost:** By entering the PIN, the victim has *authorised a debit*, not a credit. Money is immediately transferred out of their account.

## Who Is Targeted
College students (especially those selling second-hand items or awaiting scholarships), first-time UPI users, and elderly people unfamiliar with digital payments.

## Consequences
Financial loss ranging from a few hundred to several lakhs of rupees. Psychological distress, shame, and loss of trust in digital payments. In extreme cases, victims have taken drastic steps due to financial pressure.
