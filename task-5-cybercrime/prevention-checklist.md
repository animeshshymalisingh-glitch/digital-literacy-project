# Stay Safe Online – Prevention Checklist for College Students (India)

## General Online Safety

1. **Use strong, unique passwords** for every account. A strong password is at least 12 characters long and combines uppercase letters, numbers, and symbols. Use a password manager if needed.

2. **Enable two-factor authentication (2FA)** on all important accounts — email, social media, and banking apps. Even if someone steals your password, they cannot access your account without the second factor.

3. **Never click on unknown links** received via SMS, WhatsApp, or email — especially links claiming you have won a prize, received a refund, or must verify your account urgently.

4. **Verify before you trust** — if someone calls claiming to be from your bank, NPCI, or a scholarship board, hang up and call the official number yourself. Do not share OTPs, PINs, or Aadhaar numbers over the phone.

5. **Keep your devices and apps updated** — software updates patch security vulnerabilities that fraudsters actively exploit.

6. **Use only trusted, official Wi-Fi networks** — avoid logging into banking or UPI apps on public Wi-Fi at cafes, airports, or college canteens without a VPN.

---

## 💳 UPI & Financial Safety (Specific to Indian Students)

7. **Remember: entering your UPI PIN always means money leaves your account.** You do NOT need to enter your PIN to *receive* money. If anyone asks you to enter your PIN to accept a payment, it is a scam — stop immediately.

8. **Check the transaction screen carefully before approving.** UPI request screens say "PAY ₹___" not "RECEIVE ₹___". Always read the direction of the transaction. If it says "Pay," you are sending money, not receiving it.

---

## Social Media & Identity Safety

9. **Never share personal identification documents** (Aadhaar, PAN, college ID) on social media or with unverified contacts online.

10. **Report cyberbullying and harassment immediately** — do not engage with abusers. Block, screenshot for evidence, and report both to the platform and to authorities.

---

## 🚨 How to Report Cybercrime in India

| Channel | Details |
|---|---|
| **National Cyber Crime Portal** | [cybercrime.gov.in](https://cybercrime.gov.in) — file online complaints 24×7 |
| **Cyber Crime Helpline** | **1930** — available 24×7; call immediately if money is lost to freeze the transaction |
| **Local Police** | File an FIR at your nearest police station for serious cases |

> **Tip:** If you lose money to UPI fraud, call **1930 immediately** — the earlier you report, the higher the chance of blocking the transaction before funds are withdrawn.
